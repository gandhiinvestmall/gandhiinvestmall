Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a disciplined approach to investing in mutual funds. It allows you to invest a fixed amount regularly at predetermined intervals (usually monthly) in a mutual fund of your choice. SIPs provide the benefit of rupee cost averaging and the power of compounding to help you generate wealth over time.

Types of Systematic Investment Plan

  • Regular SIP

    This is the most common type of SIP, where an investor invests a fixed amount at regular intervals (monthly or quarterly) in mutual funds. Best for: Investors looking for a simple, consistent, and disciplined approach to building wealth over time.

  • Top-Up SIP

    In this SIP, you start with an initial investment amount and then periodically increase your SIP amount (usually by a fixed percentage or amount) as per your preference. Best for: Investors with increasing incomes or those who want to gradually increase their investment over time to keep pace with inflation or rising financial goals.

  • Flexible SIP

    Unlike regular SIPs where the investment amount remains fixed, flexible SIP allows the investor to change the contribution amount depending on their financial situation. This gives more flexibility to the investor. Best for: Investors who anticipate income variability or those who prefer to adjust their investments based on market conditions or personal finances.

  • Perpetual SIP

    This SIP continues indefinitely until the investor cancels it. The investor specifies the amount and the frequency (e.g., monthly), and the SIP continues until the investor chooses to stop it. Best for: Investors who want to keep investing for the long term without having to renew or revisit their investment plan regularly.

  • Trigger SIP

    Trigger SIPs are activated based on certain predefined triggers, such as the Net Asset Value (NAV) of the fund hitting a specific level or a particular market condition. Once the trigger event occurs, the SIP gets activated. Best for: Investors who want to time their investments based on market conditions, like when the market dips or reaches a certain point.
