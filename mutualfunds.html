Types of Mutual Funds

  • Equity Mutual Funds (Stock Funds)

    These funds primarily invest in stocks (equity) of companies. They are designed to generate capital appreciation over time.

  • Money Market Funds

    These funds invest in short-term, low-risk, high-liquidity instruments like treasury bills, certificates of deposit (CDs), and commercial paper. They provide low returns but are highly liquid.

  • Debt Mutual Funds (Bond Funds)

    These funds invest in fixed-income securities such as bonds, treasury bills, and other debt instruments. They aim to provide stable income through interest payments.

  • Hybrid Mutual Funds (Balanced Funds)

    These funds invest in a mix of both equities (stocks) and fixed-income securities (bonds) to provide a balance of growth and income.

  • Index Funds

    These funds aim to replicate the performance of a specific market index (e.g., S&P 500, Nifty 50). They invest in the same securities that make up the index.
