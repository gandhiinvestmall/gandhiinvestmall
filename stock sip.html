Stock SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

A Stock SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a structured and disciplined way of investing in individual stocks on a regular basis, similar to how you would invest in mutual funds through an SIP. With Stock SIP, you invest a fixed amount of money at regular intervals (monthly or quarterly) in a portfolio of stocks, regardless of market conditions. This approach allows you to build wealth over time with the power of compounding while minimizing the impact of market volatility.

Types of Stock SIP

  • Single Stock SIP

    In this type of Stock SIP, the investor selects a single stock and invests a fixed amount at regular intervals in that particular stock.Best For: Investors who have strong conviction in a specific stock or believe that a single company has long-term growth potential.

  • Multi-Stock SIP

    In this type, an investor creates a diversified portfolio of stocks and invests in multiple stocks at regular intervals. The portfolio may include large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap stocks from different sectors.

  • Sector-Specific Stock SIP

    In a sector-specific Stock SIP, an investor chooses stocks within a specific sector, such as technology, healthcare, energy, or banking. Regular investments are made into stocks that belong to the chosen sector.

  • Thematic Stock SIP

    This type of Stock SIP focuses on investing in stocks based on a particular theme or investment trend, such as electric vehicles, renewable energy, AI, or sustainability.

  • Dividend Stock SIP

    This type of Stock SIP focuses on investing in stocks that regularly pay dividends. The investor sets up SIPs in stocks with a strong track record of dividend payouts.
